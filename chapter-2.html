Chapter 2. Mathematical Foundations

This chapter reviews the mathematical background.

Definition 2.1 (Category)

A category consists of objects, morphisms, composition, and identity satisfying the associativity and identity laws.

Limits and Colimits

A limit of a diagram \(D : \mathcal{J} \to \mathcal{C}\) is a universal cone over \(D\).

Limits generalise products, pullbacks, and equalisers.

\[\lim_{\leftarrow} D \cong \int_{j \in \mathcal{J}} D(j)\](2.1)
Remark

Colimits are the dual notion, obtained by reversing all arrows.

  • Products are limits over the discrete diagram.

  • Equalisers are limits over the parallel-arrows diagram.

  • Pullbacks are limits over the cospan diagram.
